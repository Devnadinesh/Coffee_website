TYPES

Coffee beans are seeds of a fruit called coffee cherry.Coffee cherries grow on coffee trees from a genus of plants called Coffea.There are a wide variety of species of coffee plants,ranging from shrubs to trees.

  • Type of bean: There are two main types of coffee species, Arabica and Robusta.Arabica originates from Ethiopia.The main differences between them are their taste and where they are grown. When it comes to their difference in caffeine level, Robusta beans contain twice the level of caffeine than Arabica beans. Arabica beans have a milder, more aromatic taste while Robusta beans hold a stronger, more acquired flavor.


  • Type of roast: Coffee roasts can be categorized into four main types based on the degree of roasting, each offering a distinct flavor profile. Light roasts, with temperatures between 356°F and 401°F, are light brown and have bright, acidic, and complex flavors, often with fruity or floral notes. Medium roasts, ranging from 410°F to 428°F, are medium brown and provide a balanced mix of acidity and body with a hint of sweetness. Dark roasts, with temperatures from 464°F to 482°F, are dark brown to almost black, with a bold, smoky, and robust flavor, pronounced bitterness, and less acidity.


  • Type of grind Coffee grinds can be categorized into several types based on coarseness, each suited for different brewing methods. Extra coarse grind, resembling peppercorns, is used for cold brew, while coarse grind, like sea salt, is ideal for French press. Medium grind, similar to sand, suits drip coffee makers, and fine grind, like sugar, is perfect for espresso machines. Extra fine grind, almost powdery, is used for Turkish coffee.


  • Decaffeinated Coffee Decaffeinated coffee, or decaf, is coffee with most of the caffeine removed, usually through water soaking and extraction methods. It retains much of the flavor and aroma of regular coffee but with significantly less caffeine. This makes it a suitable option for those wanting to reduce their caffeine intake. A small amount of caffeine typically remains in decaf coffee.

TOP BRANDS









1. Starbucks:

Starbucks, founded in 1971 in Seattle, is a global coffeehouse chain renowned for its high-quality Arabica coffee. Known for its wide range of espresso-based drinks, innovative creations like the Frappuccino, and seasonal specialties, Starbucks emphasizes sustainable sourcing and ethical practices. The company offers both in-store experiences and products for home brewing, making it a central figure in global coffee culture.

2. Dunkin' Donuts:

Dunkin' Donuts, founded in 1950, is a popular coffee and baked goods chain known for its consistently delicious coffee. Offering a variety of coffee drinks, from classic hot and iced coffee to specialty beverages like lattes and macchiatos, Dunkin' Donuts emphasizes affordability and convenience. Its coffee is often praised for its smooth, robust flavor, making it a favorite choice for many coffee lovers.

3. McCafe:

McCafé, launched by McDonald's in 1993, offers a range of coffee beverages known for their quality and affordability. McCafé serves classic espresso drinks like lattes and cappuccinos, as well as iced and blended coffee options. Sourced from 100% Arabica beans, McCafé coffee is crafted to provide a smooth, rich flavor, making it a convenient and popular choice for coffee enthusiasts seeking a reliable cup of coffee.

4. Peet's Coffee:

Peet's Coffee, founded in 1966 in Berkeley, California, is a specialty coffee roaster and retailer known for its rich, deeply roasted beans. The company prides itself on high-quality, ethically sourced Arabica coffee, offering a variety of beverages from traditional espresso drinks to unique blends. Peet's is recognized for its commitment to freshness, with beans often roasted in small batches and shipped promptly to ensure optimal flavor. It played a crucial role in the growth of the American coffee culture and continues to be a favorite among discerning coffee enthusiasts.
